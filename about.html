So why are we doing this?

During the Summer of 2020 news media have showcased the negative effects of policing within the United States. From this, the country has sparked a debate on police's excessive force and militarization within the United States. My research is on doing a data visualization project that showcases the excessive force of police militarization in the United States. For my project, I visualized data that is centered around the police departments from 3 major cities (New York, Philadelphia, and Washington D.C). The main aim of this is to compare and contrast the resource divide within these cities, where I plan to compare governmental funding and policing funding in the hopes to tell a story as to why there is a low allocation of resources in those communities. This data visualization has an abolitionist framework advocating for a defunding of police, reduced militarization, and reallocation of resources to communities in need. Using data visualization I compare the staunch governmental divide of funding to the police compared to community-based funding like mental health resources, affordable housing infrastructures, and SNAP program recipients. Along with the data visualization, I found grassroots organizations that follow an abolitionist approach for their community within the cities I am visualizing. From this work, a database holds information on grassroots organizations and antiracist resources in the hopes to add accessibility to finding these resources as well as alleviating the need and support of the police. The technology used HTML, CSS,Node.js, D3.js, Relational Databases, Heroku, and Github.
